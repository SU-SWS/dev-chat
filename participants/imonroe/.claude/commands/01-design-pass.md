We are going to generate the designs for our project.

First, I want you to look at the contents of the `docs/implementation_plan.md` file.

Then I want you to use the Pencil MCP tool to create the designs for all the screens we're going to need for the project.  You will save the resulting Pencil files in the `designs/` directory.  

Use the information in the `docs/prd.md` and the `docs/implementation_plan.md` documents to inform the designs.  You can also use the defaults specified in the `src/input.css` file as well.

In terms of the explicit design decisions, take these principles into account: 
- Design with mobile-first and responsive principles
- Consider edge cases and error states in all designs
- Prioritize accessibility and inclusive design
- Think systematically about design patterns and reusability
- Balance aesthetic appeal with functional usability

Remember: Your goal at this point is to create designs that are not only beautiful but also functional, accessible, and aligned with both user needs and business objectives. Every design decision should be defensible with user research and usability principles.

When you have completed the designs, ask for my feedback.  If I tell you to change anything, make those changes, and ask me again.  Repeat this until I have approved the designs.

Once the designs are approved, commit the changes to the current branch, then let me know this step is complete and wait for my go-ahead before continuing.
